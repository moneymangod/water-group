A Vital Resource

What Type of Resource is Water?

Water is a unique and essential resource. It is a renewable resource, meaning it can be replenished through the natural water cycle. However, it's also a finite resource because the total amount of water on Earth remains relatively constant. It's crucial to manage it sustainably.

Places We Use Water

Domestic Use (drinking, sanitation, cooking)

Agriculture (irrigation)

Industry (manufacturing, energy production)

Transportation (rivers, canals)

Fisheries and Aquaculture

Recreation (swimming, boating)

Factors of Water Being Wasted

  • Pollution: Contamination from industrial discharge, agricultural runoff, and sewage.
  • Overconsumption: Excessive use for irrigation, industrial processes, and domestic purposes.
  • Leakage: Losses from aging and poorly maintained infrastructure.
  • Inefficient Irrigation: Traditional methods leading to significant water loss.
  • Climate Change: Altering rainfall patterns, leading to droughts and floods.

Methods to Conserve Water

Reduce Water Use

  • Shorter showers
  • Fix leaks
  • Turn off tap while brushing teeth

Rainwater Harvesting

  • Collect rainwater for gardening

Efficient Irrigation

  • Drip irrigation
  • Water scheduling

Wastewater Treatment

  • Recycle treated water

Policy and Management

  • Water pricing
  • Regulations

Education

  • Raise awareness
